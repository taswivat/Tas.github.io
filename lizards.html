Komodo dragons are a large reptile of the monitor lizard family they are large lizards with long tails, strong and agile necks, and sturdy limbs. Their tongues are yellow and forked. Adults are an almost-uniform stone color with distinct, large scales





habitat

Komodo dragons live on a few Indonesian islands in the Lesser Sunda group, primarily Komodo National Park. This includes the islands of Komodo, Rinca, Gili Dasami, Gili Montang, and Flores. They inhabit tropical savanna forests and grasslands, preferring warm, humid environments.





size

The Komodo dragon is the largest living lizard in the world. These wild dragons typically weigh about 154 pounds (70 kilograms), but the largest verified specimen reached a length of 10.3 feet (3.13 meters) and weighed 366 pounds (166 kilograms). Males tend to grow larger and bulkier than females.





Eating Habits

Komodo dragons eat almost any kind of meat, scavenging for carcasses or stalking animals that range in size from small rodents to large water buffalo. Young feed primarily on small lizards and insects, as well as snakes and birds. If they live to be 5 years old, they move onto larger prey, such as rodents, monkeys, goats, wild boars and deer (the most popular meal). These reptiles are tertiary predators at the top of their food chain.
